Data Protection Laws

Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: The data subject must be informed about the processing in a clear, fair, and lawful manner. 

Limitation on purpose: You may only handle data for the justified uses that were made clear to the data subject at the time of collection. 

Data reducing: Only gather and handle the minimal amount of information required to fulfill the stated goals. 

Accuracy: It's important to maintain current and correct personal data. 

Storage limitations: Personal identification information may only be kept for as long as it is required to fulfill the stated purpose. 

Integrity and secrecy: Processing must be carried out in a fashion that guarantees the necessary levels of security, integrity, and secret. 

Accountability: The data controller must be able to provide proof that they comply with each of these GDPR standards.
